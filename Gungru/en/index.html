Preserving a Living Tradition

Gungru Khangtsen is one of eleven monastic houses within Gaden Shartse Monastery in Mundgod, India — home to the exiled Tibetan monastic community since 1966. Our monks dedicate their lives to the study of Buddhist philosophy, debate, and meditation, preserving a tradition stretching back over six centuries. Through annual USA tours featuring sacred sand mandala creation, teachings, and traditional ceremonies, we share this wisdom with the world.

☸ What We Do

Our Mission

Dedicated to preserving and sharing the profound wisdom of Tibetan Buddhism.

📖

Monastic Education

Our monks undertake a rigorous 15–20 year program studying the Five Great Treatises through debate, memorization, and contemplation — the heart of the Gelug tradition.

🌈

Sacred Arts Tours

Each year, our monks travel to the United States to create sacred sand mandalas, perform traditional ceremonies, and share Buddhist teachings with communities nationwide.

🙏

Prayer Services

We perform Pujas (prayer ceremonies) for healing, protection, and merit. You can request prayers for yourself or loved ones, including Medicine Buddha and Tara Pujas.

🌐

Cultural Preservation

As part of the Tibetan exile community, we preserve ancient texts, philosophical traditions, ritual arts, and the Tibetan language for future generations.

💙

Community Support

We provide food, shelter, education, and healthcare for over 60 monks, many of whom are elderly or have dedicated decades to preserving the Dharma.

🍷

Butter Lamp Offerings

Light a butter lamp in your name or in memory of a loved one. The flame symbolizes the dispelling of darkness and the illumination of wisdom.
